Ways to detect Phishing 


  • Do you know the sender?


    If the message seems to come from a person or an agency you know (your boss, your friend, your bank, ...), check the email address and check if it is the same address used by the person you know, and if this address does not contain spelling mistakes. Otherwise, it is most likely a phishing message. But beware, a legitimate email address still does not offer any guarantees as to the veracity of the email.


  • Is the message misspelled?


    If the message contains spelling errors or grammatical errors, then be careful, it may be a phishing message. Even if malicious cybercriminals try to use correct language, mistakes or the use of a foreign language can be a sign of a suspicious message.


  • What to Do If You Responded to a Phishing Email



    If you think a scammer has your information, like your Social Security, credit card, or bank account number, go to IdentityTheft.gov. There you’ll see the specific steps to take based on the information that you lost.If you think you clicked on a link or opened an attachment that downloaded harmful software, update your computer’s security software. Then run a scan.


  • What if you've already fallen into the trap of a Phishisng Email?


    Do not open emails which you suspect as being scams.Do not forward emails which you suspect as being scams.Do not open attachments from unknown sources.

  •  Don’t trust the header from email address


    Fraudsters not only spoof brands in the display name, but spoof  the header from email address, including the domain name. Keep in mind that just because the sender’s email address looks legitimate (e.g sendername@yourcompany.com), it may not be. A familiar name in your inbox isn’t always who you think it is!


  • What should you do if you’ve received a scam email?

    • Do not click on any links in the scam email.
    • Do not reply to the email or contact the senders in any way.
    • If you have clicked on a link in the email, do not supply any information on the website that may open. 
    • Do not open any attachments that arrive with the email.
