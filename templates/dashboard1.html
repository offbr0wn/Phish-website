What is Phishing

  •  Phishing is sending fake unsolicited emails that ask recipients, under various pretexts, to click on a link leading to a fake website or open a malicious attachment. The hacker collects the information provided there and uses it to divert funds to his advantage. The fake website, like email, looks authentic, as it is often a certified copy of the financial institution or business site.


  • As defined by NIST , phishing is a technique for trying to acquire sensitive data, such as bank account numbers, through fraudulent solicitation by email or on a website, in which the author impersonates a legitimate business or a reputable person.


  • According to ANSSI , phishing attacks are aimed at obtaining the bank details or login credentials to financial services of the recipient of legitimate seeming emails in order to steal his money.The main reason why these attacks are so successful is the users themselves. Thus, our website aims to raise awareness of the threats of phishing to minimize losses. we suggest that you test your security by assessing your level of awareness and find out to what extent you are able to determine if an email is fraudulent.
